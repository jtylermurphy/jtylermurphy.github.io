DoD changes to the BAH will affect families who rent housing.

Your out of pocket expenses are about to go up. 2016 will mark the second phase of a five-year plan approved by Congress under which service members receiving BAH will pay an increasing percentage of average off-base housing costs.

A legislative proposal was approved in 2015 that allows the DoD to reintroduce a member cost-sharing element (out-of-pocket amount), not to exceed one percent of national average housing costs by grade. Meaning, renter's insurance was eliminated from the 2015 Basic Allowance for Housing rate computation.

On average, the cost of renter insurance contributed about $20/month to the Basic Allowance for Housing rates, with the actual amount varying by grade and location. Members who obtain renter insurance will now need to budget for this expense separately.

Similar to homeowner insurance, renter insurance protects you in situations that everyone can face: fire, theft, water damage, and many other perils.
